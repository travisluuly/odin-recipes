The Perfect Boiled Egg

The other recipes included in this website has the boiled egg as the star of the show. As such, it is critical that you first know how to boil the perfect egg to your liking.

The outcome of this recipe will vary based on how runny you want your yolk to be. The general rule of thumb is to avoid over-cooking your egg, which results in a grey/green ring around the yolk

Time

15 minutes

What you will need

  • Eggs
  • Bowl of Ice Water
  • Saucepan
  • Timer

Steps

  1. Add enough water to your saucepan to cover your eggs and bring it to a rolling boil
  2. Once your water is at a rolling boil, add your eggs to the saucepan
  3. Start your timer to keep track of how long the eggs are boiling!
  4. Once your timer reachers a certain time, you may remove your eggs and add them to your bowl of ice water. The timings will affect how well-done your eggs are and will be listed below
  5. Peel your eggs or save them to enjoy for later!

When do I remove my eggs?

  • At 6 minutes, the eggs will come out soft boiled with a liquidy yolk
  • At 8 minutes, the yolk of the egg will still be soft, but no longer liquidy or jammy
  • At 12 minutes, the egg is hard boiled and the yolk of the egg will come out firm. Perfect for egg salad!
